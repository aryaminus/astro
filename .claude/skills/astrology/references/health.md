# Medical Astrology — health, body, and the chart

**Read this for any health-related question.** This file is the focused
ruleset for medical astrology; the deeper astromedical theory and surgical

## The four health houses

Every chart has a body map distributed across the four "health houses":

| House | Western name | Vedic name | What it shows |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| **1st** | Self / Ascendant | Tanu | Constitution, vitality, the body's baseline |
| **6th** | Health / daily work | Shatru / Roga | Chronic conditions, daily-body maintenance, the service the body asks |
| **8th** | Death/rebirth / crisis | Ayur | Surgeries, hidden/chronic conditions, transformation, others' impact on the body |
| **12th** | The unconscious / institutions | Vyaya | Hospitalisation, sleep, immune system, the body's porous boundary |

A **strong 1st house** (well-aspected Ascendant and its ruler) is the
foundation of good health. **Malefics in the 6th** (Saturn, Mars, Rahu, Ketu)
describe the chronic pattern; **Saturn in the 6th** = long, structural
conditions; **Mars in the 6th or 8th** = inflammation, accidents, surgery
themes. **12th house** afflictions = hidden undermining; immune, sleep,
mental health.

## Surgery timing — the most actionable rule

This is the **single most practical use of medical astrology**. The rule:

> **Avoid elective surgery when the Moon transits the sign ruling the body part.**

| Surgery on | Avoid Moon in |
| --- | --- |
| Head, brain, eyes, face | Aries |
| Throat, thyroid, jaw | Taurus |
| Lungs, shoulders, arms, hands | Gemini |
| Chest, breasts, stomach | Cancer |
| Heart, upper back, spine | Leo |
| Abdomen, intestines, spleen | Virgo |
| Kidneys, lower back, skin | Libra |
| Reproductive organs, bladder, rectum | Scorpio |
| Hips, thighs, liver | Sagittarius |
| Knees, bones, joints, teeth | Capricorn |
| Ankles, calves, circulation | Aquarius |
| Feet, immune system | Pisces |

**Additional rules** (classical):
- **Avoid lunar eclipse** windows (2 weeks before/after) for elective procedures
- **Prefer Moon in a fixed sign** (Taurus, Leo, Scorpio, Aquarius) for stable outcomes
- **Waning Moon** preferred for surgery (less vital force "bleeding out")
- **Waxing Moon** preferred for treatments that need to "build" (long-term therapies, organ-supportive)
- **Mars afflicting Moon or the Ascendant ruler** = higher risk of complications; defer
- **Mercury direct and not combust** = the surgical team thinks clearly

**For emergency surgery**: do not delay. The Moon is irrelevant when the
alternative is worse.

## When the chronic illness will ease

The single most-asked medical question: "When will this chronic thing
improve?" The chart gives timing through:

- **Active dasha / luck-pillar of the 6th lord** = the chronic period
- **Dasha / luck-pillar of a benefic or 1st lord** = relief
- **Jupiter transiting the 6th or making a trine to the 6th lord** = relief window
- **Saturn leaving the hard aspect** (the "Saturn is moving off" moment) = the
  chronic weight begins to lift
- **Sade Sati peak ending** = a 7.5-year Saturn transit over the Moon ends; the
  health-depleting chapter closes

**Always name the end date**. *"Your current 6th-lord dasha eases in March
2027; the chronic pattern has a year and a half to run."* That certainty is
the relief.

## Vedic health layer — Ayurvedic doshas from the chart

The three doshas describe constitutional type; the dominant planets in the chart reveal them:

| Dosha | Elements | Governing planets | Constitutional traits |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| **Vata** (air + ether) | Air, Ether | Saturn, Rahu, Mercury | Thin, dry, creative, anxious, variable digestion |
| **Pitta** (fire + water) | Fire, Water | Sun, Mars, Ketu | Medium build, intense, ambitious, inflammatory tendency |
| **Kapha** (earth + water) | Earth, Water | Moon, Jupiter, Venus | Solid build, calm, loyal, prone to congestion and weight |

**Derivation**: look at the Lagna (rising sign) element + Moon sign element + the dominant planetary stellium. Fire/Mars dominant = Pitta. Air/Saturn dominant = Vata. Earth or Water + Moon/Jupiter = Kapha. If two doshas are equally strong, the person is a dual-dosha type.

**Sade Sati and health**: the peak phase (Saturn directly over natal Moon) is the most physically demanding — digestion, sleep, and immunity often suffer. Not illness; it is the body's seven-year renovation. Advise: rest, reduce excess, routine.

## The honest frame — what the agent must always say

- **Astrology complements but never replaces medicine.** A real diagnosis
  comes from a real doctor. A real prescription comes from a real
  practitioner. The chart is *one input* among many.
- **Never predict a specific disease as fixed outcome.** "Your chart shows a
  vulnerability to [system]" is helpful. "You will get [disease] in [year]"
  is fear-based and unethical.
- **Never replace medical care with chart-reading.** If a person has
  symptoms, point them to a doctor. If they have a diagnosis, support the
  treatment plan; don't offer an "alternative."
- **Real medical crisis overrides the chart.** For acute symptoms
  (chest pain, sudden neurological changes, severe injury, mental health
  emergency), the right answer is "call your doctor / go to the ER / call
  the crisis line *now*," not "let me look at your 6th house."
- **The chart's gift is timing, not diagnosis.** "Don't schedule the
  surgery this week — the Moon is in the sign ruling the body part" is
  real and useful. "You have cancer" is not astrology's job.
- **Offer the constructive practice, not the verdict.** *"Your chart
  suggests watching the [system]; here are the classical supports."*

## Reading recipe

1. Read the **1st, 6th, 8th, 12th** houses and their signs and rulers
2. Identify the **planetary afflictions** (Saturn / Mars / Rahu in the health
   houses, or afflicting the Ascendant ruler)
3. Map the afflicted body **system** (the sign's ruled region)
4. Find the **timing window** for relief: dasha, transit, Sade Sati end
5. Give the **practical supports**: diet for the sign, lifestyle, surgery
   timing rule
6. **Never** the diagnosis. **Always** the pattern + the timing + the practice.
7. **Always** end with the "go see a real doctor" invitation if symptoms are
   real
