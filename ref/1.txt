## 1. The Universal Archive (Everything)

If you want the raw, translated texts of the world's spiritual and esoteric systems, there is one undisputed king of the internet.

* **Internet Sacred Text Archive (sacred-texts.com):** This is the largest free archive of public-domain books and PDFs on the web. It holds the complete English translations of the Hindu Vedas, Buddhist Sutras, the I Ching (Chinese Book of Changes), and hundreds of texts on Western Esotericism and ancient astrology.

## 2. Vedic & Hindu Astrology (Jyotisha)

Vedic astrology is a sidereal system deeply tied to Hindu philosophy. Unlike Western astrology, it focuses heavily on **Karma**, the Lunar Nodes (Rahu and Ketu), and *Dashas* (planetary periods that dictate specific life events).

* **VedAstro (vedastro.org):** A completely free, open-source project that digitizes Vedic astrology. It offers a massive library of classical texts (like the *Brihat Parashara Hora Shastra*), and because it is open-source, it provides highly accurate API and markdown-friendly data structures for calculating life events and karma connections using NASA JPL ephemeris data.
* **VedicAstrologer.org:** Maintained by P.V.R. Narasimha Rao, this site offers his comprehensive textbook, *Vedic Astrology: An Integrated Approach*, as a free PDF. It is widely considered one of the best English introductions to mathematical Vedic astrology and karmic calculation.

## 3. Chinese Astrology & Feng Shui

Chinese astrology is built on the Lunar Calendar, the Five Elements (Wu Xing), and the concept of spatial harmony.

* **YourChineseAstrology.com:** An excellent, highly detailed free resource for calculating your **BaZi** (Four Pillars of Destiny). It provides extensive, free write-ups on the Lunar Calendar, elemental alignments, and how to navigate yearly **Tai Sui** (Grand Duke Jupiter) clashes.
* **Joey Yap's Free Resources:** Joey Yap is a leading modern authority on Chinese Metaphysics. While he sells premium courses, his mastery academy website offers excellent free foundational PDFs and videos on BaZi and classical Feng Shui.

## 4. Western Astrology & Horoscopes

Western astrology uses the tropical zodiac and focuses heavily on psychological insight, personality analysis, and the Sun signs.

* **Astrodienst (astro.com):** Universally recognized by professional astrologers as the gold standard for free Western horoscopes, birth charts, and transit tracking. Their "Astro Databank" is a massive, wiki-style repository of verified birth data and chart analyses.

## 5. Markdown & Open-Source Calculation Engines

Since you specifically requested Markdown resources, you might be interested in the underlying calculation engines that power these charts. If you want raw data to generate your own markdown reports or build custom workflows, these open-source repositories are the best available:

* **Stellium (GitHub):** A modern Python library built on the Swiss Ephemeris. It generates highly accurate SVG charts and can extract structured markdown data for Western, Vedic, and Chinese astrology natively.
* **Kerykeion (GitHub):** Another robust, data-driven Python engine that calculates exact planetary positions, aspects, and transits, making it incredibly easy to export astrological data into Markdown or JSON for custom projects.
